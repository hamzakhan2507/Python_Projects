# Project Summary

This project analyzes vacant residential properties in Syracuse, New York, using Open Data Syracuse 2024 vacant property data and U.S. Census ACS demographic indicators.

The project was completed by Hamza Khan and Eunhae Uhm for Open Data Syracuse 2024.

## Focus

The analysis asks whether vacant properties are evenly distributed across Syracuse or concentrated in specific areas, and whether those patterns overlap with demographic and socioeconomic indicators.

## Main finding

Vacant residential properties were spatially concentrated, with more than half located in ZIP codes 13205 and 13204.

The GIS maps showed visible overlap between higher-vacancy areas and areas with larger African American and people of color populations. The project also compared vacancy patterns with median income and median household income.

## Interpretation

This is exploratory spatial analysis. The maps show patterns and possible equity concerns, but they do not prove causation.

## Recommendations

1. Prioritize vacancy interventions in ZIP codes 13205 and 13204.
2. Use targeted rehabilitation incentives to bring vacant properties back into productive use.
3. Combine vacancy analysis with demographic and income data before designing housing interventions.
4. Continue monitoring vacancy patterns using open data.
